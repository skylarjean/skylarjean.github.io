## About Me
  
I'm Skylar Callis, a researcher in mathematics, an artist, and a generally curious person. 

### Research and Education

I'm currently a graduate student working on my joint M.S. in Data Science and Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics at the University of Delaware, with an estimated graduation date of June 2030.

I have two bachelors degrees from Michigan Technological University--- one in Civil Engineering and one in Applied Mathematics. I started doing research with their [Enterprise program](https://www.mtu.edu/enterprise/) during my second year of undergrad, and fell in love with research. Some additional internship experience sold me on a career doing research, and shifted my focus from engineering to mathematics. 

>"The best thing about being a statistician is that you get to play in everyone's backyard." - [John Tukey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Tukey)

If you're interested in learning more about my research, check out my [[research/index|Research Gallery]] for the projects I'm most proud of.

If you want more detail about where I've worked and what I've published, you can view my [[resume/index|Resume & CV]].

## Purposes of This Site

This site was built to solve a handful of problems:
1. I was paying Squarespace way too much money to host an essentially static copy of my resume;
2.  I was tired of googling the same three questions about anaconda over and over again;
3. I wanted to establish a system to write, store, and index research notes ahead of writing my PhD thesis; and,
4. I had made notes/resources on a variety of unrelated topics that I wanted to make publicly available somehow.

So, I built something that is equally for me as it is for others. 

### The Digital Garden

I stumbled across the idea of digital gardening while doing research for an internship. I had been thinking about problem 3 above, and was smitten with the idea of a living, breathing home for my notes. As I let the idea stew more, I realized it could also solve problems 2 and 4. Hence, it became the driver for the development of this site.

My digital garden is where you can find all of my notes, on any topic I have thought it worthwhile to make notes on. You can read more about the [[digital-garden|philosophy and practices of my digital garden]]; or read about [[site-setup|how I set it up]].

### The Blog

The blog here was inspired by one of my close friends in graduate school who kept a Tumblr blog about her experiences. This blog featured reflection and round-up posts that summarized her experiences each semester, both the good and the bad. I don't have a very good short-term memory, and often struggle to remember what I have done in a given day, week, or month. I have developed systems for this on a daily basis, but I was attracted to the idea of longer-term reflections.
